Pixels

are the basic building blocks

of digital images

they are the

smallest

.

controllable thing

on the screen

(smaller than the dot)

the word Pixel is made of

pix

for pictures

&

el

for element

roughly, a pixel is:

an element in a picture

pixels can appear as

different shapes

square

(the usual)

circle

line

remember that an image is only a

rectangular image grid

and that grid is only a set of

locations

(one for each point in the picture)

&

colors

(one for each location)

thats abstract, but it gets worse:

pixels have no shape of their own

think of it like this:

each pixel is a container

every time a device wants to show an image

it has to do what is called rendering

(rendering simplified) step 1

create a rectangle

(thats the image)

(rendering simplified) step 2

cut rectangle into a grid

(one space per pixel)

(rendering simplified) step 3

change each space into a shape

(in this case, a circle)

now for every location

it finds the pixel that belongs

(in the image)

and for each pixel

it asks:

"Oi! Gimme your color!"

(like a cockney)

and the pixel replies:

99

red

92

green

208

blue

=

Purple

#635CD0

bzst beep boop

(because they don't actually speak)

(rendering simplified) step 4

fill each shape with that pixel's color

(remember our purple pixel?)

don't worry, the device will do all of it automatically for you

you just have to tell the pixels what color they should carry

Pixel Art

is the process of creating images

by working at the pixel level

like drawing

one dot at a time

or like being a good old cockney bloke and

shouting at each pixel:

"Oi! Your color is Blue!"

(this doesn't actually work)

pixel art is used by most

video and mobile phone games

for example

Megaman, Zelda, & Mario

the difficulty of pixel art is

communicating precisely

using limited space and color

i.e. blurring is not cool

pixel art starts with either

 

Line Art

lines are laid out first

or

Blocking

color areas are laid out first

pixel art uses

Cartoon Shading

using a small number of shades

and some may add

Dithering

using noise to blend areas of color

pixel art usually comes in two varieties:

 

Isometric

strange 3D

or

Non-Isometric

normal 2D

isometric pixel art

implies 3D space

(using diagonal lines at a 1:2 vertical:horizontal ratio)

isometric pixel art

has no perspective

(it is a type of parallel projection)

isometric pixel art

has tricky rules

(diagonal corners have 3 pixels, while vertical corners have only 1)

non-isometric pixel art

is simply 2D

unlike most raster graphic images

pixel art scales well

both up and down

when working on pixel art, always use

nearest-neighbor or none

interpolation for scaling

check out how different interpolation works:

Nearest-Neighbor

preserves hard edges

Bilinear / Bicubic

smooths colors

speaking of hard edges

always save pixel art as

Lossless

GIF or PNG

and not as

Lossy

JPG
